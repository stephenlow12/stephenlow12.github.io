Baycrest Innovation Cart

Design for Health and Wellness

This was a collaboration project between OCAD University and Baycrest Hospital to design a mobile innovation cart. This cart would be used to facilitate design thinking research and workshops to different stakeholders throughout Baycrest. This project was done in partnership with Joyce Chang and Tracy Yang.

The Challenge

The intent of the Innovation Cart project is to provide a mobile unit that would help facilitate projects and research conducted by the Innovation Lab at Baycrest. This cart will be brought to different environments throughout Baycrest, where a variety of people, from clinicians to clients, would interact with it to develop and evaluate innovative products, technologies and processes.

User Needs Analysis

The users who are benefiting from the use of the innovation cart can be divided into three distinct groups:

Facilitators, who will use the cart to facilitate workshops, conversations, and innovation sessions. The cart will act as a portable innovation lab, to help create and capture information.

Older clients, who interact with the Baycrest Healthcare system. Thy are experts on the human factor interactions surrounding the processes and services offered.

Subject Matter Experts, who are the point-of-care workers and researchers at Baycrest; the cart will be utilized to educate and capture their feedback and gather their group wisdom.

Ideation

From our journey maps, we crafteda list of features that the cart must, should, and could have. For our initial ideation, we focused on extendable, large, table spaces to better facilitate sessions for larger groups. This however would not only increase the size and weight of the cart, but also block access to storage space.

After user testing our initial prototype with our stakeholders, we were able to conclude the following about our initial ideation:

  • Environmental considerations to operate large table surfaces.
  • Adjustable surfaces to better suite environments.
  • Large storage area could have potential for future prototypes.
  • Discretion of dual use table and whiteboard/display for simultaneously usage.

Prototyping

After a second round of feedback from our stakeholders, we were able to finalize our product and start the physical 1:1 scaled prototype.

Key Learnings

Do not become too attached to ideas. Your first idea, no matter how great you think it is, will require some change. Keep your user(s) in mind at the back of your head, no matter how good you think the design works for yourself. For most projects you won't necessarily be the user, but always empathize and advocate for the user.

This was a pivotal project in helping me identify what I was truly passionate about. I realized from working countless hours in the woodshop, that this was not my "cup of tea", but that I still very much enjoyed the design process and formulating solutions for the end user.
