AIR MILES Rewards Program

UX Design Intern

I joined the AIR MILES Business Transformation team, reporting to the AD of User Experience, and also got to work closely with the User Research team. I was curious to see how the team here designed their AIR MILES product portfolio to cater to 11 million collectors, in an increasingly competitive loyalty market.

The Challenges

Since my team was under shared services, I was able to work on a variety of different projects. I learned a lot about the different design and research processes available, and when it was appropriate to use each process. In terms of products, I got to conceptualize a new Chat Landing page, in place of the outdated one. I also got to conceptualize the redesign for CELO Green, the software used on every call by our customer agents.

Under the UX Research team, I had the opportunity to recruit, moderate, and conduct, user research sessions with both internal stakeholders, as well as our collectors. I looked at opportunities to improve the Offers workstream, and offer collectors a better omni-channel experience, synthesized the data for the navigation of the upcoming AIR MILES site relaunch, and looked at the best way to present Perks for our collectors. I also helped with the user research and synthesize the findings for our national promotion, Shop the Block, looking for opportunities to improve the mobile and desktop micro-site experience.

Chat Landing Page

One of the many projects I got to work on was for the Chat Landing page. Not only was it outdated, but due to a change in one of our redemption processes, the page had to redirect users to our vendor's support page to complete a certain type of redemption. The high-level goals were to:

  • • Direct users to the correct vendor support pages.
  • • Promote use of self-serve options before starting a live chat/call.
  • • Not hinder or make it difficult for users to start a live chat.
  • • Follow the current AIR MILES pattern library and branding guidelines.

Initial Research

Through the UX Research team, we found that a majority of tickets to our call centre are for PIN resets, card replacements, and email changes. All of these issues can be resolved fairly quickly with our self-serve tool. Though the chat page currently has links to the tool, it is hidden behind a mouse scrollover, and also doesn't prompt with relevant and highly visible FAQs.

Potential Solutions

These tools should be easily accessible to proactively solve our collectors' issues, prior to them needing to open a chat. Since we released Myles the FAQ Chatbot earlier this year, it would be ideal to introduce this feature to our collectors on this page as well. Myles can help guide them through their self-serve journey, or steer them towards knowledge articles, without the hassle of a chat or call.

Another feature to consider is the call-to-action button that opens the chat. There are currently six "Chat Now" CTA buttons, which might overwhelm someone looking to just change their PIN. This goes hand-in-hand with the wording of the self-serve copy as well. Phrases like “How do I …” that don’t relate to the specific task, makes it more frustrating to scan for the one solution relevant to them.
