AIR MILES Rewards Program

UX Design Intern

I joined the AIR MILES Business Transformation team, reporting to the AD of User Experience, and also got to work closely with the User Research team. I was curious to see how the team here designed their AIR MILES product portfolio to cater to 11 million collectors, in an increasingly competitive loyalty market.

The Challenge(s)

Since my team was under shared services, I was able to work on a variety of different projects. I learned a lot about the different design and research processes available, and when to use which process. In terms of products, I got to conceptualize a new Chat Landing page, to replace the outdated one. This page also had to direct collectors to the appropriate vendor support pages, due to a new method of redemption with our partners.

Under the UX Research team, I had the oppurtunity to recruit, moderate, and conduct, user research sessions with both internal stakeholders, as well as our collectors. We looked at ways to improve the Offers workstream, through giving our collectors a better onmi-channel experience.

I also got to work on the user research and synthesis for our national promo, Shop the Block, looking for oppurtunities to improve the mobile and desktop micro-site experience. As a side project I got to design this funky poster to help guide stakeholders in our viewing rooms during user-testing sessions.

Chat Landing Page

The current Chat Landing page needed an overhaul, as not only was it outdated, but had to redirect users looking for a certain support page to an exteneral support page. The high level goals were to:

  1. Direct users to the correct vendor support pages.
  2. Promote use of self-serve options before starting a live chat/call.
  3. Not hinder or make it difficult for users to start a live chat.
  4. Follow the current AIR MILES pattern library and branding guidelines.

Ideation

Our client came to us with a rather broad problem. Through research, it was determined that people who travel also want to meet new people, but currently 58% of people reported as 'unhappy' or 'satisfactory' with their ability to 'spontaneously meet new people'.
